Nine Men's Morris

Board

Basic Information

Nine Men's Morris is a board game for two players

It is played on a board with concentric squares

The squares are connected by lines in the middle of the sides

Each square has 8 spots, the corners and the middle of the sides

The spots are marked with a circle

Other Boards

Boards can have a different number of squares

Aside the typical 2 squared board

However, the number of spots per square is always 8

The connections between squares are in the middle of the sides

In the examples bellow,

the boards have only 2, 3 and 4 squares, respectively

Pieces

On a board with n squares, each player has 3 * n pieces

Each player plays with pieces of a different color from the opponent's

All pieces are always visible, on the board or off the board

On the board, the pieces are placed on the spots

Mills

A sequence of 3 pieces of the same color is a mill

The pieces of a mill must be along a single line

In vertical or horizontal lines, never in diagonals

The highlighted blue pieces on the board bellow are part of a mill

Game Phases

Initial Layout

Players choose a color and decide who plays first

Initially, the board is empty:

The game has 2 phases:

Phase 1 - Drop the pieces (drop)

Phase 2 - Move the pieces (move)

[Phase] Place Pieces

Players alternately place one of their pieces on a free spot

Mills can be created (but it is not advantageous)

When all pieces are placed, this phase ends

[Phase] Move Pieces

This phase begins right after all the pieces are placed

Each player alternately moves one of their pieces

A piece can only be moved to an adjacent spot

Spots must be connected by a vertical or horizontal line

[Phase] Capture Pieces

Creating a mill allows you to capture an opponent's piece

Only one piece is removed per turn

A piece can return to the position of the previous move

And form a mill again on the next move

[Phase] Open Moves

It begins when a player has only 3 pieces

That player is allowed to move to any free spot

They are not restricted to moving to adjacent spots

[Phase] End Game

The game ends with the defeat of the player who has only 2 pieces

It ends in a draw if no valid move can be made

OR

Both players have 3 pieces and after 10 moves, there is no winner
